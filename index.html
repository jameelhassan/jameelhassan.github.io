Jameel Hassan

I am a research assistant at the Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, University of Peradeniya. I also work in the COVID research group at University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka. I focus on research related to two domains; computer vision and smart grids. I graduated from the University of Peradeniya with a B.Sc specializing in Electrical & Electronics Engineering in July 2020.

Throughout my undergraduate studies, I was fortunate to be advised by many fantastic researchers. Currently I am working with the faculty in the Covid Research group on the use computer vision techniques for COVID-19 related applications and threat mitigation. I am also conducting research related to Smart Grids under supervision of Prof. Janaka Ekanayake, Dr. Roshan Godaliyadda and Dr. Parakrama Ekanayake. For my final year undergraduate research project, I worked on time series solar prediction and designed an optimised scheduling algorithm for power dispatch to a micro-grid based on the stochastic solar power predictions. This project was supervised by Prof. Janaka Ekanayake, Dr. Roshan Godaliyadda and Dr. Parakrama Ekanayake.

Research

I'm passionate about the intersection of computer vision and autonomous systems. Currently I am working on a cross domain research of computer vision and epidemiology to identify contacts to generate a contact heat map using CCTV footage only. I also focus on research related to Smart Grids in which I am solving an optimization problem to utilise active and reactive power from the PV inverter to mitigate voltage violations due to PV penetration in the Low Voltage electricity grid.

I am working on the design of a human-human interaction detection system to identify human interactions and provide a threat level assessment for a given location/environement, in the context of COVID-19. A deep learning approach is undertaken to initially identify and localise handshake interactions amongst people. This information is then abstracted into a graph and then processed to measure the threat level of the given scene and/or video.
