Research

I'm passionate about research at the intersection of computer vision, machine learning and autonomous systems. I have worked on a cross domain research of computer vision and epidemiology to identify social distancing violations such as proximity, interactions and group behaviou using CCTV footages, and on Smart Grids to mitigate voltage violations due to PV penetration in the Low Voltage electricity grid using optimization of PV inverter powers.

For my final year undergraduate research project, I worked on time series solar prediction and designed an optimized scheduling algorithm for power dispatch to a micro-grid based on the stochastic solar power predictions. It also demonstrated the performance of the Modified Auto Regressive model in comparison to LSTM based deep learning model in solar power prediction for small time horizons. This project was supervised by Prof. Janaka Ekanayake, Dr. Roshan Godaliyadda and Dr. Parakrama Ekanayake.

Research on the design of a human-human interaction detection system to identify human interactions and provide a threat level assessment for a given environement, in the context of COVID-19. A computer vision approach is undertaken to identify and localize handshake interactions, mask wearing and group behavior amongst people. This information is then abstracted into a temporal graph and then processed to measure the threat level of the given scene and/or video.
Paper Under Review at Sustainable Cities and Society, Elsevier journal.
